# Security Policy

At **MyCitadel**, security is not a feature; it is our foundational architecture. We operate on a "Default-Deny" principle and maintain a strictly hardened infrastructure. We take the reporting of security vulnerabilities with the highest level of professional seriousness.

## Reporting a Vulnerability

If you believe you have discovered a vulnerability in the MyCitadel architecture, please contact us immediately. We request that you provide us with a reasonable amount of time to investigate and remediate the issue before disclosing it to the public.

### How to Submit
To maintain the integrity of our communication perimeter, please follow these steps:

1. **Do not** open a public issue or discussion for security vulnerabilities.
2. **Contact:** Reach out via our designated secure communication channel: [Insert Secure Email or Encrypted Contact Link Here].
3. **Report Contents:**
    * A clear, concise description of the threat vector.
    * Steps to reproduce the exploit within our `Near Zero-Knowledge (N-NZK)` framework.
    * Evidence of the impact on data isolation or client-side sovereignty.

## Our Security Commitments

* **Rapid Response:** We prioritize the triage of all submitted security reports over non-critical functional bugs.
* **Non-Retaliation:** We promise not to take legal action against security researchers who report vulnerabilities in good faith and adhere to these responsible disclosure guidelines.
* **Integrity:** Every report is reviewed by our engineering team to ensure the "Default-Deny" posture of the Citadel remains uncompromised.

## Scope of Security

We focus our security efforts on the following:
* **Near Zero-Knowledge (N-NZK) Integrity:** Ensuring the mathematical sovereignty of client-side operations.
* **Data Transit:** Preventing interception of blinded communication bridges.
* **Storage Hardening:** Ensuring data remains outside the web root.

## Exclusions
The following areas are generally out-of-scope for security reporting:
* Theoretical vulnerabilities without a functional proof-of-concept.
* Issues regarding third-party external services (which we explicitly avoid).
* UI/UX aesthetics that do not impact the underlying security protocol.
